Tribute to Linus Torvalds

The creator of Linux and Git

About Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds, born in 1969 in Finland, is a software engineer best known for creating the Linux kernel in 1991. Linux powers millions of servers, smartphones, and devices around the world. He also created Git, a tool that changed how developers collaborate.

Achievements

  • Created the Linux kernel
  • Developed Git version control system
  • Won the Millennium Technology Prize in 2012
  • Influenced open-source software worldwide

Why I Admire Him

Linus Torvalds inspires me because of his dedication to open-source software. His work shows how collaboration and sharing knowledge can change the world. I admire his technical skills and the impact he has made on computing.
